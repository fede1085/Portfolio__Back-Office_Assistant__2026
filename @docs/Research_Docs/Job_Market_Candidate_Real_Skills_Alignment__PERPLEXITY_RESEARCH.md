# Research objective:

Align a candidate’s real skills with actual job roles demanded in the Antwerp (Belgium) labour market for entry-level office and administrative work.

Context of the candidate:

The candidate is based in Antwerp and is looking to enter the labour market as soon as possible. The goal is not a high-level professional role (engineering, consulting, architecture, etc.) but an operational office role.

The candidate profile:

• Strong digital literacy
• Advanced user of Microsoft 365 tools (Word, Excel, Teams, SharePoint)
• Comfortable with digital tools and technology in general
• Able to assist colleagues with software and digital workflows
• Basic understanding of AI tools and modern digital workflows
• Strong organizational mindset and ability to assist teams

Limitations:

• No formal degree in Belgium
• No Microsoft certifications
• Self-taught technical knowledge
• Functional English, but no Dutch or French
• Limited formal office experience in Belgium

Strategic positioning:

The goal is to position the candidate as a **digital office assistant / back-office support profile**, not as an IT specialist or consultant.

A profile similar to:

• Administrative Assistant with strong digital skills
• Back Office Assistant
• Office Assistant
• Sales Support
• Operations Assistant
• Customer Support Officer
• Digital Office Support

The candidate wants to enter in a **practical operational role**, demonstrate value inside the company, and then grow internally.

Research tasks:

1. Identify the **real job titles used in Antwerp and Flanders** for entry-level office roles that match this profile.
2. Analyze job listings to determine **the most common titles used by companies** for administrative / back-office roles.
3. Extract **the skills most frequently requested** for these roles.
4. Identify **which roles are accessible without formal certifications or degrees**.
5. Identify roles where **digital literacy and Microsoft tools are valued but not formally required**.
6. Identify roles commonly used as **entry points into office environments**.
7. Identify roles where **English may be sufficient in international companies**.

Requested output:

Provide a structured analysis including:

1. The **10–20 most common job titles** used for these roles in Antwerp/Flanders.
2. Typical **skills required for these jobs**.
3. Which roles are **most realistic for someone without certifications**.
4. Roles where **digital skills provide a competitive advantage**.
5. Job titles that best **match the candidate’s profile**.

Goal:

Help translate the candidate’s real abilities into **job titles and roles that actually exist in the Antwerp job market**, so the profile can be aligned with what employers are actively searching for.

Important: Use dutch language if it is needed in your research, to get better results, your output, your answer must be in english language.

Here is a detailed report that maps your digital office profile to the real administrative and back-office job titles and requirements currently used in Antwerp and Flanders. It covers:

- The 10–20 most common job titles for entry-level office roles
- The skills employers request most often
- Which roles are realistically accessible without degrees or certifications
- Where strong digital and Microsoft 365 skills give you a clear advantage
- The job titles that best fit your current profile, plus how to position yourself on CV/LinkedIn
